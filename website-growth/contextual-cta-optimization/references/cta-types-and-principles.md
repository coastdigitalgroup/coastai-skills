# The 3 Types of Contextual CTAs

To maximize conversion without ruining the reading experience, use a mix of these three CTA styles based on the content flow.

## 1. The Inline Anchor (The "Intent Bridge")
Descriptive text links woven directly into the narrative.

*   **Best for:** Maintaining high reading flow and "passive" conversion.
*   **Placement:** Within paragraphs, usually after identifying a specific problem or desired outcome.
*   **Psychology:** Uses the principle of **Proximity**. The solution is offered at the exact moment the problem is mentioned.
*   **Example:** *"Once you've [defined your goals], you can use a [Goal Setting Template] to track your progress."*

## 2. The Pattern Break (The "Attention Grabber")
A visually distinct block (box, banner, or callout) that separates sections of text.

*   **Best for:** Highlighting high-value resources (Checklists, Whitepapers, Webinars).
*   **Placement:** After a major sub-section (H2) or about 50% through the article.
*   **Psychology:** Uses the **Von Restorff Effect** (the isolation effect). Because it looks different from the surrounding text, the brain prioritizes it.
*   **Example:** A box with a light background and a bold headline: *"Stop Guessing. Get the ROI Calculator."*

## 3. The Contextual Slide-In (The "Relevance Trigger")
A small UI element that slides in from the corner after a specific user action (scroll depth or time on page).

*   **Best for:** Capturing high-intent users who are actively engaged with the content.
*   **Placement:** Triggered after 50-70% scroll depth.
*   **Psychology:** Leverages **Reciprocity**. The user has already received significant value from the content, making them more likely to agree to a "next step."
*   **Constraint:** Ensure it is easily dismissible and does not block the main text on mobile.

## Persuasion Principles for Contextual CTAs

### Reciprocity
Always give before you ask. Informational content provides the "Value" (the gift). The CTA is the "Ask." If the value of the gift is high, the conversion rate on the ask will follow.

### Specificity
Generic CTAs like "Sign up for our newsletter" are weak because they are vague. Contextual CTAs like "Get the Project Planning Checklist" are strong because they promise a specific, immediate result.

### Awareness Level Alignment
*   **Top of Funnel (TOFU):** Offer more education (Checklists, Guides).
*   **Middle of Funnel (MOFU):** Offer proof (Case Studies, Comparison Guides).
*   **Bottom of Funnel (BOFU):** Offer action (Trials, Demos, Pricing).
