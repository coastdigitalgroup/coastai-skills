# Example: Identifying Pricing Friction with Micro-Surveys

This example demonstrates how a B2B SaaS company used a micro-survey to uncover why users were dropping off from their pricing page, leading to a 12% lift in trial signups.

## The Problem

A project management software company noticed that 75% of users who reached the pricing page exited without clicking any CTA. Quantitative data (Heatmaps) showed users were spending significant time looking at the "Enterprise" tier but not clicking "Contact Sales."

## The Skill Application (Micro-Survey)

The team deployed an **Exit-Intent Micro-Survey** specifically on the pricing page for desktop users.

### Survey Configuration:
- **Trigger:** Exit-intent (mouse-out).
- **Question:** "Quick question: What's the one thing that nearly stopped you from starting a trial today?"
- **Options:**
  - "The price is too high"
  - "I'm not sure which plan I need"
  - "I'm missing a specific feature"
  - "Just browsing / Not ready yet"
  - "Other (please specify)"

### The Findings:
Over 14 days, the survey received 450 responses.
- **42%** of respondents chose: **"I'm not sure which plan I need."**
- The qualitative "Other" responses revealed that users were confused about whether the "Pro" plan included a specific "Gantt Chart" feature, which was only mentioned deep in a comparison table.

## The Fix (Optimization)

Based on these insights, the team made two changes to the pricing page:
1. **Plan Selector Guide:** Added a small "Help me choose" tooltip that mapped team size to the recommended plan.
2. **Feature Highlighting:** Explicitly added "Includes Gantt Charts" to the top 3 bullet points of the Pro plan card.

## The Outcome (Measurable Results)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pricing Page CVR** | 4.2% | 4.7% | **+11.9%** |
| **Exit-Intent Response Rate** | N/A | 2.8% | N/A |
| **"Help me choose" Engagement** | N/A | 15% | N/A |

**Key Insight:** By asking "Why," the team discovered that the barrier wasn't the price itself, but the *clarity of value* within the pricing tiers.
