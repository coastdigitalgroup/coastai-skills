# Case Study Optimization Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate existing customer success stories and identify
friction points that are killing conversion.

## 1. The "PSR" Structural Integrity
- [ ] **Problem Clear?** Is the initial business pain described in a way that
  a prospect can relate to?
- [ ] **Solution Specific?** Does it explain *how* the product solved the pain,
  rather than just listing features?
- [ ] **Result Quantifiable?** Are there real numbers (%, $, hours) included
  to prove success?
- [ ] **Protagonist Check:** Is the customer the "hero" of the story, or is it
  just an ad for the company?

## 2. Scannability & Visual Hierarchy
- [ ] **Outcome Header:** Does the page title include a result/benefit?
- [ ] **Summary Bar:** Is there a "Stats at a Glance" bar above the fold?
- [ ] **Sub-headers:** Are sub-headers benefit-driven rather than labels
  (e.g., "Saving 10 hours" vs "The Result")?
- [ ] **Strategic Bolding:** Is the most important data (numbers, KPIs) bolded
  to catch the eye of skimmers?

## 3. Trust & Authenticity
- [ ] **Real Person:** Is the testimonial attributed to a real person with a
  name, title, and (ideally) a photo?
- [ ] **Identifiable Company:** Is the company name and industry clearly
  stated?
- [ ] **Verifiable Metrics:** Do the results sound realistic, or are they
  generic "marketing-speak"?
- [ ] **Direct Quotes:** Are quotes conversational and emotive, rather than
  stiff and corporate?

## 4. Conversion Pathing
- [ ] **Primary CTA:** Is there a clear primary action at the end of the story?
- [ ] **Contextual CTAs:** Are there "micro-CTAs" placed after major result
  highlights?
- [ ] **Zero Dead-Ends:** Does the page offer "Next Steps" or related case
  studies to keep the user in the funnel?
- [ ] **Low-Friction Option:** If the main CTA is "Book a Demo," is there a
  lower-friction secondary option (e.g., "Download PDF version")?
