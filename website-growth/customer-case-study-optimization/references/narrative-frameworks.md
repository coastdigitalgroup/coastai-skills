# Narrative Frameworks for Conversion

To optimize a case study, you must move from "storytelling" to "proof-building." The following frameworks and principles are the foundation of high-conversion case studies.

## 1. The PSR Framework (Problem-Solution-Result)

This is the gold standard for business case studies. It follows a logical progression that mirrors the customer's buying journey.

| Phase | Purpose | Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Problem** | Establish Empathy | Focus on the business impact of the pain point. Use the "Cost of Inaction" (COI) to build urgency. |
| **Solution** | Demonstrate Capability | Focus on the *process* and the *selection criteria*. Why was this the right fit? |
| **Result** | Provide Proof | Focus on quantifiable, verifiable outcomes. Lead with hard metrics (ROI, Efficiency, Revenue). |

## 2. The "Hero's Journey" (Adapted for B2B)

In a conversion-focused case study, the **Customer** is the Hero, and the **Product/Service** is the Guide.

1.  **The Ordinary World:** The customer's status quo before the problem became unbearable.
2.  **The Call to Adventure:** The realization that the status quo is no longer sustainable.
3.  **Meeting the Guide:** The customer discovers your solution.
4.  **The Road Back:** The implementation process and overcoming initial hurdles.
5.  **Return with the Elixir:** The final result and the new, improved status quo.

## 3. Persuasion Principles for Case Studies

*   **Social Proof:** People look to the actions of others to determine their own. A case study is the ultimate form of specific social proof.
*   **The Specificity Effect:** Specific numbers (e.g., "24.7%") are more believable than rounded numbers (e.g., "25%") or vague claims ("nearly doubled").
*   **The Peak-End Rule:** People judge an experience largely based on how they felt at its peak and at its end. In a case study, ensure the "Peak" (the solution) and the "End" (the result) are the most memorable parts.
*   **Relatability Bias:** Prospects are more likely to be persuaded by customers who share their industry, size, or specific challenges.

## 4. Copywriting Heuristics

*   **Active Voice:** Use "The team increased sales" instead of "Sales were increased by the team."
*   **Benefit-Led Subheadlines:** Every subheadline should pass the "So What?" test.
    *   *Bad:* "Our Implementation Process"
    *   *Better:* "Getting Up and Running in 48 Hours"
*   **The "Rule of Three":** When listing results or features, groups of three are more memorable and persuasive.
