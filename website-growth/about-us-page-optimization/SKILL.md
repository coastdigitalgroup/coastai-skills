# About Us Page Optimization

## Purpose

The About Us Page Optimization skill provides a systematic framework for
transforming a standard "company history" page into a high-impact trust asset.
While often overlooked, the About page is a critical destination for users in
the "Evaluation" phase who are looking for reasons to trust a brand. This skill
refines the narrative to be customer-centric, highlights authority and
expertise, and ensures that the page serves as a bridge to conversion rather
than a dead-end.

## Use Cases

- B2B companies where the "person behind the brand" is a key decision factor.
- E-commerce brands with a unique mission or origin story that justifies premium
  pricing.
- Startups needing to build immediate credibility in a crowded market.
- Non-profits or mission-driven organizations where the "Why" is the primary
  conversion driver.
- Professional services (agencies, consultants) where trust in expertise is
  paramount.

## When NOT to Use

- **Product Detail Pages (PDPs):** These should focus on specific item benefits.
  Use `product-page-optimization`.
- **Purely Technical Documentation:** Where the user is looking for "How-to"
  rather than "Who."
- **High-Volume Commodity Retail:** Where brand story is secondary to price and
  shipping speed.
- **Support/Help Centers:** Where the goal is utility and problem resolution.

## Inputs

1. **Founding Story & Mission:** The original "Why" and the problem the company
   was built to solve.
2. **Key Personnel/Team Data:** Professional bios, photos, and unique expertise.
3. **Proof of Authority:** Awards, certifications, media mentions, or years of
   experience.
4. **Analytics:** Traffic volume to the About page, bounce rate, and
   next-page-path (where do they go after?).
5. **Customer Persona Anxiety:** What do users fear about companies in this
   space (e.g., "Will they disappear in 6 months?", "Are they actually
   experts?").

## Outputs

1. **About Page Audit:** Identification of "ego-centric" copy and missing trust
   signals.
2. **Revised Narrative Framework:** A customer-centric story that connects the
   brand's mission to the user's needs.
3. **Authority & Trust Map:** Strategic placement of bios, certifications, and
   milestones.
4. **CTA Strategy:** Integration of soft or hard conversion points to keep users
   in the funnel.

## Workflow

### 1. The "Ego-to-Empathy" Pivot

Audit the current copy. If it starts with "We were founded in 1994..." it's too
ego-centric.
- **The Flip:** Reframe the story around the problem you solve for the user.
- **Before:** "We have 20 years of experience in accounting."
- **After:** "For 20 years, we've helped small businesses navigate complex tax
  laws so they can focus on growth."

### 2. Humanize the Brand

Users trust people, not logos.
- **Real Faces:** Replace stock photos or generic office shots with high-quality
  photos of the actual team.
- **Expertise Bios:** Ensure team bios highlight *why* their experience helps
  the customer, not just their education.
- **The "Behind the Scenes":** Use visuals that show the "work in progress" to
  build authenticity.

### 3. Layer in Authority (The Trust Stack)

Systematically place proof points throughout the narrative:
- **Milestones:** Use a timeline that emphasizes growth and stability.
- **Badges & Certifications:** Place industry-specific trust signals near key
  claims.
- **Media Proof:** "As seen in..." logos or links to significant press coverage.

### 4. Optimize the "Next Step"

The About page should never be a dead-end.
- **Soft CTAs:** "Meet the Team," "Read Our Blog," or "Join the Newsletter."
- **Hard CTAs:** "See Our Services" or "Start Your Trial" for users who have
  found the trust they needed.
- **Strategic Placement:** Place a primary CTA at the bottom of the page and
  secondary links within the narrative.

### 5. Review Against Decision Rules

Verify that the narrative remains scannable and persuasive.

## Decision Rules

- **The 80/20 Rule:** 80% of the About page should be about how the company's
  experience/mission benefits the *customer*. Only 20% should be "pure" history.
- **Visual Evidence > Claims:** Don't just say you're a "global team"—show a
  photo of the team or a map of your impact.
- **No Stock People:** If you don't have a photo of a real person, use an
  illustration or a text-based authority signal. Stock photos of "smiling
   professionals" destroy trust.
- **The Scannable Story:** Use bold headers and pull-quotes so a user can get
  the "vibe" of the brand in 10 seconds of scrolling.

## Constraints

- **Factual Integrity:** All claims regarding company history, awards, and team
  credentials must be accurate and verifiable.
- **Brand Voice Alignment:** The narrative must match the tone established on
  the rest of the site (e.g., don't be "stuffy" on the About page if the product
  is "playful").
- **Asset Availability:** The quality of this optimization is limited by the
  availability of real team photos and authentic brand assets.

## Non-Goals

- Writing full team bios or executive profiles from scratch.
- Creating a comprehensive brand style guide or mission statement.
- Managing PR or external media outreach.

## Common Failure Patterns

- **The History Museum:** A dry, boring list of dates that provides no value to
   a potential customer.
- **The "We Are" Trap:** Starting every sentence with "We," "Our," or "Us,"
  ignoring the user's needs.
- **Invisible People:** No photos or names of the leadership team, making the
  brand feel like a "faceless corporation."
- **The Dead-End Page:** No CTA at the bottom, forcing the user to use the main
  nav or leave.
- **Generic Vibe:** Using corporate jargon like "world-class solutions" or
  "innovative paradigms" that say nothing.

## Validation Criteria

- [ ] **Next-Page Progression:** Measure the percentage of users who move from
  the About page to a Product or Pricing page.
- [ ] **Bounce Rate Reduction:** A decrease in exits from the About page
  indicates higher engagement.
- [ ] **Trust Sentiment:** Qualitative feedback from user testing: "After
  reading this page, how much do you trust this company?"
- [ ] **Form Conversion:** If a "soft" CTA (like a newsletter) is added, measure
  its opt-in rate.
