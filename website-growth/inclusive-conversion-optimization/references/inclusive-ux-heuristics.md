# Reference: Inclusive UX Heuristics

When optimizing for growth through inclusivity, apply these core heuristics to
ensure the experience is robust for the widest possible audience.

## 1. The Heuristic of Sensory Redundancy
**Principle:** Never rely on a single sensory channel (sight, sound, or color)
to communicate critical information or a primary action.
- **Conversion Application:** If a field is required, don't just make the label
  red. Add an asterisk (*) or the word "Required." If a sale is ending, use a
  visual timer AND a text-based countdown.

## 2. The Heuristic of Cognitive Ease
**Principle:** Minimize the "mental math" and memory required to complete a
task.
- **Conversion Application:** Use descriptive button text like "Start My Free
  Trial" instead of "Continue." Avoid complex jargon in the hero section. In
  checkouts, show the total price (including shipping) as early as possible.

## 3. The Heuristic of Interaction Forgiveness
**Principle:** Design for human error and situational distraction.
- **Conversion Application:** Provide large touch targets (min 44x44px). Allow
  users to go back and edit form steps without losing data. Use "Smart
  Defaults" for fields like City/State based on Zip Code to reduce typing.

## 4. The Heuristic of Spatial Predictability
**Principle:** Maintain a logical, consistent layout that follows standard web
patterns.
- **Conversion Application:** Keep the "Cart" in the top right. Put "Submit"
  at the bottom of the form. Keyboard focus should follow the visual order of
  the page (top-to-bottom, left-to-right).

## 5. The Heuristic of Environmental Robustness
**Principle:** Ensure the interface is usable in sub-optimal physical
conditions (glare, noise, slow internet).
- **Conversion Application:** High contrast ratios (4.5:1+) aren't just for
  vision impairment; they allow a user to shop on their phone while standing in
  direct sunlight. Captions allow a user to convert from a video while in a
  noisy airport or a quiet library.

## 6. The Heuristic of "Affordance Clarity"
**Principle:** Every interactive element must look and behave like its
function.
- **Conversion Application:** Buttons should look like buttons (with depth or
  clear borders). Links should be underlined or distinctly colored. If a user
  can't tell what is clickable, they can't convert.
