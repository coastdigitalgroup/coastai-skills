# Example: Identifying Pricing Friction with Micro-Surveys

This example demonstrates how a B2B SaaS company used a targeted micro-survey to identify why users were bouncing from their pricing page, leading to a significant conversion lift.

## The Problem (Before)

- **Scenario:** A project management software company noticed that 65% of visitors who landed on the pricing page exited without clicking "Start Free Trial."
- **Current Data:** Google Analytics showed high exit rates on the pricing page, but heatmaps showed users were scrolling all the way to the bottom, indicating they were reading but not acting.
- **Metric:** 3.2% Click-Through Rate (CTR) from Pricing to Signup.

## The Intervention

The team implemented an exit-intent micro-survey specifically on the pricing page.

### Survey Configuration
- **Trigger:** Exit-intent (mouse-out toward browser bar).
- **Targeting:** New visitors who have spent at least 15 seconds on the page.
- **The Question:** "Is there anything on this page that isn't clear?"
- **Options:**
  - [ ] Pricing tiers
  - [ ] Specific features
  - [ ] Integration options
  - [ ] Something else (please specify)

### Findings
Over two weeks, 450 users responded (7% response rate).
- **42%** chose "Specific features."
- **Open-ended follow-up:** "What feature were you looking for?"
- **Result:** Over 120 users mentioned "Gantt Charts." Gantt charts were included in the "Pro" plan, but they weren't explicitly listed in the pricing table—only on a separate "Features" page.

## The Solution (After)

- **The Change:** The team updated the pricing table to explicitly include "Gantt Charts" as a bullet point in the Pro and Enterprise tiers.
- **Secondary Change:** Added a small "View All Features" link that opened a comparison modal, keeping users on the pricing page.

## Measurable Outcome

- **CTR Lift:** The CTR from the Pricing page to the Signup page increased from **3.2% to 4.8%** (a 50% relative increase).
- **Trial Volume:** Monthly trial signups increased by **18%**.
- **Customer Feedback:** A follow-up survey on the signup page showed a 30% reduction in "Lack of information" as a reason for hesitation.
