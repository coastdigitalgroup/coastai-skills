# Discount & Coupon Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate the effectiveness and profitability of your
current promotional strategy.

## 1. Strategy & Framing (The "Rule of 100")
- [ ] Are items **under $100** framed as percentage discounts (e.g., "25% Off")?
- [ ] Are items **over $100** framed as absolute dollar amounts (e.g., "$50 Off")?
- [ ] Is the primary benefit (the savings) the most prominent part of the
  promotional copy?

## 2. AOV & Tiered Rewards
- [ ] Is there a "Free Shipping" threshold clearly visible in the cart?
- [ ] Is the "Free Shipping" threshold set at least 15-20% higher than your
  current Average Order Value?
- [ ] Do you use tiered rewards (e.g., "Spend $X, Save $Y") to encourage larger
  baskets?
- [ ] Is there a visual progress bar or "Nudge" message showing how close the
  user is to the next reward?

## 3. Checkout UI & UX
- [ ] Is the "Promo Code" field subordinated (collapsed or behind a link) to
  prevent users from leaving to search for coupons?
- [ ] If a discount is auto-applied (e.g., via URL), is there a clear "Success"
  message in the cart?
- [ ] Does the "Discount" line item in the order summary show the total saved in
  a high-contrast color (e.g., Green)?
- [ ] Are error messages for expired or invalid codes helpful rather than
  frustrating?

## 4. Profitability & Margin Guardrails
- [ ] Have you calculated the "Break-Even Conversion Lift" required to justify
  the current discount depth?
- [ ] Are you avoiding "Coupon Stacking" (preventing multiple high-value
  discounts from being used together)?
- [ ] Are the exclusions (e.g., "Not valid on Sale items") clearly stated but
  not overwhelming?
- [ ] Is there a plan to "Retire" underperforming coupons every 30-90 days?

## 5. Behavioral Triggers
- [ ] Is there an **Exit-Intent** discount for first-time visitors with items
  in their cart?
- [ ] Do you offer "Post-Purchase" upsell discounts that don't require
  re-entering payment info?
- [ ] Are you using "Time-Limited" urgency triggers (e.g., "Code expires in 2
  hours") for cart recovery?
