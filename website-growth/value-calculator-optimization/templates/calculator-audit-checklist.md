# Value Calculator Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate and optimize interactive ROI, Savings, or
Estimation tools.

## 1. Friction & Flow
- [ ] **No Early Lead Wall:** Does the user get to the first question without
  providing contact info?
- [ ] **Low Entry Barrier:** Is the first question a simple "Slider" or "Toggle"?
- [ ] **Sensible Defaults:** Are industry-standard averages pre-filled for
  complex fields?
- [ ] **Logical Steps:** Are 5+ questions broken into 2-3 logical steps?
- [ ] **Progress Indicator:** Is there a clear "Step X of Y" or progress bar?

## 2. Calculation Logic
- [ ] **Transparency:** Is there a "How we calculate this" link or modal?
- [ ] **Credibility:** Are the benchmarks or data sources cited?
- [ ] **Realism:** Does the math produce believable results (not "too good to
  be true")?
- [ ] **Instant Feedback:** Does a "Total" counter update as the user moves
  sliders?

## 3. Results & Persuasion
- [ ] **Visual Impact:** Are results shown via charts or graphs (not just
  text)?
- [ ] **Primary Metric:** Is the "Winning Number" (e.g., ROI) the largest
  element on the results page?
- [ ] **Before vs. After:** Is the contrast between the "Current State" and
  "Optimized State" clear?
- [ ] **Shareability:** Is there a one-click "Email these results" or "Copy Link"
  button?

## 4. Conversion Strategy
- [ ] **The "Value Exchange":** Is the lead form tied to a higher-value output
  (e.g., "Get the full PDF report")?
- [ ] **Contextual CTA:** Is the primary button benefit-driven (e.g., "Schedule
  my Savings Review") rather than generic ("Contact Us")?
- [ ] **Proximity:** Is the CTA placed immediately next to the primary result?

## 5. Mobile & Technical UX
- [ ] **Thumb-Friendly:** Are sliders and buttons at least 44px tall?
- [ ] **No Keyboard Clash:** Do inputs use the correct `type` (e.g., `number`)
  to trigger the right mobile keyboard?
- [ ] **Speed:** Does the calculator load and calculate in < 500ms?
- [ ] **Responsive Charts:** Do graphs re-format or scroll horizontally on
  small screens?
