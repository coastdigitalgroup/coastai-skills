# Contact Experience Heuristics

These heuristics guide the design of a contact experience that builds trust and maximizes sales velocity.

## 1. The Speed-to-Trust Correlation
Research shows that lead conversion drops by **10x** if the initial response takes longer than 5 minutes. While 5 minutes is difficult for email, managing expectations is the surrogate for speed.
- **Heuristic:** If you cannot be fast, you must be precise. Tell the user *exactly* when they will hear back.

## 2. The "Physical Anchor" Rule
Even for 100% remote or digital companies, a physical address acts as a psychological anchor for trust.
- **Heuristic:** A contact page without a physical address or a headquarters location is perceived as "higher risk" by enterprise buyers and cautious consumers.

## 3. Intent Symmetry
The data you request in a form should be proportional to the value of the outcome.
- **Sales/Quote:** High value -> High data (Budget, Timeline, Company Size).
- **General Inquiry:** Low value -> Low data (Name, Email, Message).
- **Support:** High resolution -> High precision (Order ID, Error Message).

## 4. The "Success Bridge"
The moment after submission is a "Trust Peak." Do not waste it with a blank "Success" page.
- **Heuristic:** Use the thank-you page to bridge the user to the next logical step.
  - *Sales:* Provide a case study or "What to expect on our call" guide.
  - *Support:* Provide a link to the Knowledge Base.

## 5. Visual Hierarchy of Paths
The "Sales" path should always be the "Path of Least Resistance."
- **Heuristic:** If a user wants to buy, they should never have to scroll past support links or "Media Inquiries" to find the "Request a Quote" button.

## 6. The "Human in the Loop" Pattern
Show the user *who* they are talking to.
- **Heuristic:** Replacing a generic "The Team" with "Sarah, Head of Customer Success" or a photo of the actual office increases form completion rates by up to 20%.
