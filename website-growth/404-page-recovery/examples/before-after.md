# Before and After: 404 Page Recovery

## Scenario: RetroGear E-commerce

RetroGear is a vintage-inspired apparel store. They recently migrated to a new platform and have hundreds of old links from Pinterest and fashion blogs that now lead to 404 errors.

### The "Before" (Dead End)
The original 404 page was the platform default.

- **Content:** A giant "404" heading, a small "Page not found" subheader, and a "Return to Home" button.
- **User Experience:** Users arriving from a specific Pinterest pin for "Leather Jackets" land on a page that tells them the product is gone. They have to click "Home" and then find the search bar or navigate manually.
- **Outcome:** 85% Bounce Rate.

### The "After" (Discovery Hub)
The 404 page was optimized using the **404 Page Recovery** skill.

- **Copy:** "Oops! That specific piece has left the shop, but we have something even better for you."
- **Primary Tool:** A prominent search bar with the placeholder "What are you looking for?"
- **Contextual Fallback:** Since many 404s came from jacket-related links, the page now features a "Trending Now" grid specifically showing the current Leather Jacket collection.
- **Low-Friction Offer:** A "Sorry about that" banner offering a 10% discount code for their next order.
- **Measurable Outcome:**
    - **Bounce Rate:** Dropped from 85% to 42%.
    - **Recovery Rate:** 58% of users continued to a product page.
    - **Revenue Recovery:** $2,400 in "lost" sales recovered in the first 30 days via the 10% discount code used directly from the 404 page.
