# Case Study Optimization: Before and After

This example demonstrates the transformation of a standard "Project Story" into a high-conversion "Business Case Study" for a B2B SaaS company specializing in Inventory Management.

## Scenario: "Logistics Pro" SaaS

Logistics Pro had a case study about their client, "Global Parts Co." The original version was a 1,200-word blog post that focused heavily on the software's features.

### Before: The "Chronological Story"

*   **Title:** How Global Parts Co. Implemented Logistics Pro
*   **Structure:** Introduction -> Our Features -> The Implementation Process -> Conclusion
*   **Key Content:**
    *   Detailed description of the API integration.
    *   Paragraphs about the training sessions held with staff.
    *   A quote from the IT Manager: "The software works well and the team is helpful."
*   **Outcome:**
    *   **Average Time on Page:** 45 seconds (users skimmed and left).
    *   **Conversion Rate (to Demo):** 0.4%.
    *   **Sales Feedback:** "Clients find it too technical; it doesn't help me close deals."

---

### After: The "PSR Framework" Optimization

The case study was restructured using the **Problem-Solution-Result** framework, optimized for a VP of Operations persona.

*   **Title:** How Global Parts Co. Reduced Inventory Overhead by 22% in 6 Months
*   **Structure:** Executive Summary -> The Problem (Legacy Bottlenecks) -> The Solution (Automated Reconciliation) -> The Result (ROI & Efficiency)
*   **Key Content:**
    *   **Executive Summary:** A sidebar at the top with three bullets:
        1.  **Problem:** $1.2M in annual loss due to manual stock counting errors.
        2.  **Solution:** Real-time sync across 4 warehouses via Logistics Pro.
        3.  **Result:** 22% reduction in overhead; $300k saved in first 180 days.
    *   **Visual Proof:** A chart showing the downward trend of "Lost Inventory" after month 3 of implementation.
    *   **The "Impact" Quote:** A pull-quote from the VP of Operations: "Logistics Pro paid for itself in one quarter. We no longer guess our stock levels; we know them."
    *   **Contextual CTA:** A mid-page button: "Calculate your potential inventory savings [Link to Calculator]."

*   **Measured Outcome:**
    *   **Average Time on Page:** 2 minutes 15 seconds (users engaged with charts and summary).
    *   **Conversion Rate (to Demo):** 3.8% (nearly 10x increase).
    *   **Sales Feedback:** "This is my go-to document for enterprise leads."

## Why it Worked

1.  **Outcome-First Title:** The title shifted from a "how-to" to a "value-received" statement.
2.  **Immediate ROI:** The executive summary allowed busy decision-makers to see the value without reading 1,000 words.
3.  **Specific Metrics:** Moving from "it works well" to "$300k saved" provided the logic needed for a purchase approval.
4.  **Persona Alignment:** By focusing on overhead and ROI rather than API details, the content spoke directly to the person with the budget.
