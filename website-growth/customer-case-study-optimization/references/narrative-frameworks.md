# Narrative Frameworks for Customer Success

To transform a case study into a conversion asset, you must choose a narrative framework that aligns with how B2B buyers make decisions.

## 1. The PSR Framework (Problem-Solution-Result)
The most effective framework for growth-focused case studies. It follows a logical, evidence-based progression.

*   **Problem:** The "Status Quo" was no longer sustainable. Define the pain, the cost, and the frustration.
*   **Solution:** The "Turning Point." Why was your product chosen? How did it bridge the gap?
*   **Result:** The "New Reality." Quantifiable proof of success. ROI, time saved, or emotional relief (peace of mind).

## 2. The Hero's Journey (Adapted for B2B)
Useful for long-form, brand-building case studies where the *customer* is the hero and your *product* is the "magical tool" or "mentor."

1.  **The Call to Adventure:** The customer realizes they need to change (e.g., a market shift or internal failure).
2.  **Meeting the Mentor:** They find your product/service.
3.  **Crossing the Threshold:** The implementation phase (overcoming initial hurdles).
4.  **The Ordeal:** Addressing the core challenge using your tool.
5.  **The Reward:** Achieving the goal and returning to "normal business" with new powers (efficiency/growth).

## 3. The "Before vs. After" Comparison
Best for visual products (UI/UX, Interior Design, Physical Goods) or high-data products (Performance Marketing, SEO).

*   **Side-by-Side:** Show a screenshot of the old dashboard vs. the new one.
*   **Metric Comparison:** A table showing "Old Process Time" vs. "New Process Time."
*   **The "Gap":** Explicitly label the improvement (e.g., "+45% Efficiency").

## 4. The Industry Benchmark Framework
Best for establishing authority in a specific niche.

*   **The Challenge:** "Most companies in [Industry] struggle with [Common Problem]."
*   **The Benchmark:** "[Customer Name] was performing at industry average (X)."
*   **The Outperformance:** "After implementing [Product], they now perform 3x better than the benchmark."

---

## Persuasion Principles to Apply

*   **Social Proof:** "If it worked for a company like mine, it will work for me."
*   **Authority:** Using recognized brand logos and professional titles.
*   **Specificty:** "14.2% increase" is more believable than "About 15%."
*   **Risk Reversal:** Showing that others have already "taken the leap" and succeeded.
