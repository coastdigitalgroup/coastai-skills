# Viral Mechanics & Referral Principles

Understanding the underlying mechanics of viral growth is essential for optimizing referral loops.

## 1. The Viral Coefficient (K-factor)
The K-factor measures the number of new users generated by each existing user.
- **Formula:** `K = (average number of invitations sent per user) * (conversion rate of those invitations)`
- **Interpretation:**
    - `K > 1`: Exponential growth (Viral).
    - `K = 1`: Linear, steady growth.
    - `K < 1`: Viral decay (requires external marketing to sustain growth).

## 2. Viral Cycle Time
The time it takes for a user to go from signing up to inviting another user.
- **Principle:** The shorter the cycle time, the faster the growth.
- **Optimization:** Trigger referral prompts as early as the "Aha! Moment" rather than waiting for weeks of usage.

## 3. Incentive Psychology
- **Loss Aversion:** Framing a reward as something the user "has already earned" but will lose if they don't share (e.g., "You have a $20 credit pending - invite a friend to unlock it").
- **The Altruism Effect:** People are often more likely to share if they feel they are *helping* a friend rather than just gaining a reward for themselves.
- **Social Currency:** Users share things that make them look good, smart, or "in the know" (e.g., "I have 3 exclusive invites to this private beta").

## 4. Network Effects
Referral loops are most powerful when the product itself becomes more valuable as more people join (e.g., Slack, WhatsApp).
- **Direct Network Effect:** Increased usage of the product by others increases the value for the original user.
- **Marketplace Effect:** More sellers attract more buyers, which attracts more sellers (e.g., Airbnb, Etsy).

## 5. Trust Mechanics
- **Implicit Recommendation:** A referral link from a friend carries significantly more weight than a paid advertisement.
- **Brand Transfer:** The trust the referee has in their friend is transferred to the brand the friend recommends.
