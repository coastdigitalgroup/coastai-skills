# Video Persuasion Principles

These heuristics guide the creation and optimization of high-converting video
assets.

## 1. The "Information Scent" Continuity
The video should feel like a seamless extension of the ad or link that brought
the user to the page. If the ad promised "Faster Reporting," the video thumbnail
and the first 3 seconds must mention "Faster Reporting."

## 2. The Pattern Interrupt
Online attention is fragile. Every 5–10 seconds, the video should change
visually (a cut, a zoom, a text overlay, or a new speaker) to "reset" the user's
attention and prevent passive scrolling.

## 3. The "Silent Cinema" Principle
Up to 80% of social and web video is consumed without sound. If your video
requires audio to be understood, it will fail for the majority of users. Use:
- **Burned-in Captions:** (Open captions) that cannot be turned off.
- **Graphic Overlays:** To highlight key statistics or benefits.
- **Visual Storytelling:** Showing the product in action rather than just a
  "talking head."

## 4. The "Micro-Moment" of Choice
Users decide whether to stay or leave in the first **3 seconds**.
- **0s - 3s:** The Hook (Validate intent).
- **3s - 15s:** The Promise (What they will learn/get).
- **15s+:** The Evidence (The "Meat" of the content).

## 5. Risk Reversal via Video
Video is the ultimate trust builder. Use it to lower the "Perceived Risk":
- **Face-to-Camera:** Builds human connection.
- **Unedited Demos:** "Raw" screen recordings can sometimes convert better than
  high-polish animations because they feel more "real" and "trustworthy."
- **Customer Voices:** Real customers speaking in their own environment carry
  more weight than a professional voiceover actor.

## 6. The "Aha!" Milestone
Every video should have a specific "Aha!" moment—the point where the user
realizes how the product solves their specific pain. Identify this moment and
ensure it happens in the first 30% of the video's duration.
