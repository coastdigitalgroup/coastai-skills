# Eugene Schwartz's Five Levels of Awareness

Understanding the Five Levels of Awareness (from the classic book *Breakthrough Advertising*) is the foundation of User Intent Alignment.

### 1. Unaware
The user doesn't even know they have a problem.
- **Strategy:** Interrupt and educate. Focus on the "Cost of Inaction" or a shocking insight.
- **Goal:** Move to Problem Aware.

### 2. Problem Aware
The user knows they have a pain point but doesn't know there is a solution.
- **Strategy:** Empathize with the pain. Use "How-to" content and educational guides.
- **Goal:** Move to Solution Aware.

### 3. Solution Aware
The user knows solutions exist (e.g., they know "CRM software" is a thing) but hasn't picked your product.
- **Strategy:** Differentiate and compare. Show why your *category* or *method* is the best.
- **Goal:** Move to Product Aware.

### 4. Product Aware
The user knows your product but isn't sure it's the right fit for them.
- **Strategy:** Provide proof and specific features. Use case studies, testimonials, and detailed specs.
- **Goal:** Move to Most Aware.

### 5. Most Aware
The user knows your product, likes it, and just needs the final "deal."
- **Strategy:** Remove friction. Focus on price, guarantees, and a seamless checkout experience.
- **Goal:** Conversion.
