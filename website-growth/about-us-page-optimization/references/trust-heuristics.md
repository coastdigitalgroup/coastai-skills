# Trust Heuristics for Brand Storytelling

Building trust on an "About Us" page requires balancing emotional connection
with rational authority. Use these heuristics to guide the optimization
process.

## 1. The Principle of Radical Transparency
In an era of AI-generated content and "fake" brands, transparency is a
competitive advantage.
- **Show the Messy Middle:** Don't just show the polished final product; show
  photos of the office, the team working, or the manufacturing process.
- **Own the Mistakes:** If the brand was founded because the creator was
  frustrated by a failure in the industry, mention it. It builds empathy.

## 2. The Authority-Similarity Paradox
Users want to buy from people who are "like them" but also "better than them"
(experts).
- **Similarity:** Use language that mirrors the customer's world. If selling to
  skiers, use skier terminology.
- **Authority:** Counterbalance similarity with hard proof of expertise:
  titles, years of experience, or specific technical achievements.

## 3. The "Social Glue" of Mission
A shared mission creates a "tribe" around a brand.
- **Value Alignment:** Clearly state what the company believes in (e.g.,
  "We believe privacy is a human right").
- **External Impact:** Show how the company's existence makes the world
  better beyond just selling a product.

## 4. Visual Trust Signals (The "Gaze" Effect)
- **Eye Contact:** In team photos, subjects looking directly at the camera build
  more trust than those looking away.
- **Consistency:** Ensure team photos have a consistent lighting and style to
  convey a professional, unified organization.

## 5. The Peak-End Rule on the About Page
Users remember the "Peak" (the most emotional part of the story) and the "End"
of their experience on the page.
- **The Peak:** Make the origin story or the mission statement the most
  visually and emotionally resonant part of the page.
- **The End:** Ensure the final CTA feels like a helpful invitation, not a
  forced sales pitch.
