# Case Study Narrative Frameworks

To optimize a case study, you must move from "Journalism" (reporting what
happened) to "Conversion Storytelling" (persuading others it will happen for
them).

## The PSR Framework (Core)

The Problem-Solution-Result (PSR) framework is the standard for high-conversion
business proof.

### 1. The Problem (The Villain)
Don't just say they had a problem. Describe the **cost of inaction**.
- **Financial Cost:** "Losing $10k/month in manual errors."
- **Opportunity Cost:** "The engineering team spent 40% of their time on
  maintenance instead of new features."
- **Psychological Cost:** "The team felt burnt out by the inefficient workflow."

### 2. The Solution (The Tool)
Focus on the "Bridge." How did they get from the Problem to the Result?
- Avoid the "Feature Dump."
- Connect specific features to the problems they solved.
- *Example:* "Instead of manually tagging leads (Problem), they used our
  **Automated Lead Scoring** (Feature) to prioritize high-intent users
  (Benefit)."

### 3. The Result (The Hero)
The result must be **Verifiable**, **Relevant**, and **Comparative**.
- **Verifiable:** "Verified by 3rd party audit" or signed by the Head of Sales.
- **Relevant:** If you're selling to CFOs, show money. If selling to CEOs, show
  market share.
- **Comparative:** "A 25% improvement compared to their previous provider."

## The "Hero's Journey" for B2B

While PSR is the structure, the "Hero's Journey" is the emotional hook.

1. **The Hero:** Your customer (NOT your product).
2. **The Call to Adventure:** The moment they realized the status quo was no
   longer working.
3. **The Guide:** Your product/service. You are the mentor providing the map and
   the tool.
4. **The Transformation:** How their business (or their day-to-day life) has
   fundamentally changed for the better.

## Trust Heuristics for Case Studies

- **The Specificity Heuristic:** Humans find "14.2%" more believable than "15%"
  or "About 15%."
- **The Relatability Heuristic:** A visitor is 3x more likely to convert after
  reading a case study about a company *in their own industry*.
- **The Authority Heuristic:** Featuring a recognizable brand logo (even for a
  smaller project) builds "Halo Effect" trust for the rest of your site.
- **The Aesthetic-Usability Effect:** A case study that is beautiful and easy to
  scan is perceived as more "accurate" and "professional" than a plain-text
  document.
