# Wishlist UX Heuristics & Persuasion Principles

Optimizing a wishlist is about managing the psychological transition from
"browsing" to "buying." Use these principles to guide your implementation.

## 1. Recognition Over Recall
Users should not have to remember why they saved an item.
- **Contextual Meta-data:** Show the date added or the category the user was
  browsing when they saved it.
- **Visual Cues:** Use the same "Hero" image the user saw on the PLP/PDP to
  trigger instant recognition.

## 2. The Endowment Effect
Once a user "saves" an item, they begin to feel a sense of ownership over it.
- **Messaging:** Use "Your Saved Items" or "My Collection" instead of "Generic
  Wishlist."
- **Nudges:** Frame notifications as "Don't lose your item" rather than "Buy
  this now."

## 3. The Zeigarnik Effect
Unfinished tasks create a mental tension that is only relieved upon completion.
- **Progress Indicators:** Show "You have 3 items waiting in your wishlist."
- **Visual Reminders:** A "Heart" icon with a number badge acts as a persistent
  reminder of an "unfinished" shopping task.

## 4. Reducing Interaction Cost
The value of a wishlist is its speed.
- **Micro-interactions:** A single click should save an item. Any more (like
  selecting a folder or adding a note) should be optional and secondary.
- **Bulk Actions:** Allow users to "Move all to cart" or "Delete all out of
  stock" to manage larger lists effortlessly.

## 5. Trust & Persistence
If a user saves 10 items and they disappear when they refresh the page, trust
is destroyed.
- **Cross-Device Continuity:** While anonymous saving is good for friction,
  gently encourage account creation (e.g., "Save these items forever and view
  them on your phone") to ensure long-term persistence.
- **Privacy First:** Ensure wishlist data is handled according to privacy
  standards, especially for shared lists.

## 6. Scarcity & Urgency
Use the wishlist as a "Personalized Urgency" feed.
- **Custom Alerts:** A generic "Sale ends tonight" banner is less effective than
  a "The [Product Name] in your wishlist is now 20% off for the next 4 hours"
  alert.
