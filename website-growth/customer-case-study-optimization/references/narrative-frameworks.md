# Narrative Frameworks for High-Conversion Case Studies

## 1. The PSR Framework (Problem-Solution-Result)

This is the gold standard for growth-oriented case studies. It moves the reader from empathy (Problem) to curiosity (Solution) to conviction (Result).

### Phase 1: The Problem (The "Pain")
- **Context:** Describe the customer's world before the solution.
- **The Trigger:** Why did they seek help *now*? What changed?
- **Impact:** What was the business cost of the problem? (Lost revenue, high churn, wasted time).

### Phase 2: The Solution (The "How")
- **Selection:** Why did they choose you over competitors?
- **Implementation:** How was the solution deployed? (Keep this focused on strategy, not just a list of buttons clicked).
- **The Unique Mechanism:** Highlight the specific feature or methodology that made the difference.

### Phase 3: The Result (The "Win")
- **Hard Metrics:** Use the "Before vs. After" format. (e.g., "From 10 leads to 50 leads").
- **Qualitative ROI:** The human impact. (e.g., "The marketing team no longer spends Sundays doing manual reporting").
- **Future State:** How is the customer better positioned for the future?

## 2. The "Hero's Journey" Adaptation

Best for brand-heavy or "Transformational" stories where the customer is the hero.

1.  **The Ordinary World:** The customer's baseline state.
2.  **The Call to Adventure:** A new challenge or market shift.
3.  **Meeting the Mentor:** Your product/service enters the story.
4.  **Crossing the Threshold:** The implementation phase.
5.  **The Supreme Ordeal:** Overcoming the technical or organizational hurdles.
6.  **The Reward:** The successful outcome and the new "Better Life."

---

## 3. The "Comparison" Framework

Best for case studies where the primary goal is to win against a specific competitor.

- **The Old Way:** How the customer used to work (often using a competitor's tool).
- **The Friction:** Specific limitations of the "Old Way."
- **The Switch:** Why they moved to your solution.
- **The New Way:** How life is better/faster/cheaper now.
