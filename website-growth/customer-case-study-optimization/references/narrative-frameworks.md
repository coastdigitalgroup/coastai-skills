# Narrative Frameworks for High-Conversion Case Studies

To maximize conversion, case studies must move beyond storytelling into "Solution Mapping." Use these frameworks to structure the narrative based on user intent.

## 1. The PSR Framework (Problem-Solution-Result)
The standard for B2B SaaS. It establishes empathy, demonstrates capability, and provides proof.

1.  **Problem (The Pain):**
    - *Focus:* The cost of the status quo.
    - *Metric:* Time lost, revenue leaked, or risk incurred.
2.  **Solution (The Bridge):**
    - *Focus:* Why this specific solution was chosen over others.
    - *Action:* The "Aha! Moment" during implementation.
3.  **Result (The Proof):**
    - *Focus:* Quantifiable improvement.
    - *Metric:* ROI, efficiency gains, or growth.

## 2. The "Executive Summary" Pattern
For high-level decision-makers (C-Suite, VPs) who have limited time and need "Bottom Line" validation.

- **Outcome Header:** [Action] + [Metric] + [Timeframe] (e.g., "Reduced Churn by 15% in 60 Days").
- **The "Three Bullet" Summary:**
    - Primary Pain Point solved.
    - Core Feature used.
    - Hard ROI figure.
- **The "Direct Path" CTA:** Immediate link to pricing or sales.

## 3. The "Hero's Journey" (B2B Edition)
For complex, multi-stakeholder products where "cultural buy-in" or "ease of use" is a major objection.

- **The Hero:** The Internal Champion at the customer company.
- **The Villain:** The legacy system or fragmented process.
- **The Mentor (Your Product):** Provides the tools to overcome the villain.
- **The Transformation:** How the team's daily life improved (Reduced stress, better collaboration).

## 4. The "Feature Spotlight" Flow
For technical products where the user is looking for proof of a *specific* capability.

- **Header:** "How [Company] uses [Feature X] to [Achieve Outcome Y]."
- **Demonstration:** Screenshots or GIFs of the feature in the customer's real-world environment.
- **Validation:** Technical data proving the feature works as advertised.

---

## Decision Logic: Which Framework to Use?

| User Awareness | Goal | Recommended Framework |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Aware** | Empathy & Solution Discovery | PSR Framework |
| **Solution Aware** | Competitive Differentiation | Feature Spotlight |
| **High Intent** | Final Validation / Budget Approval | Executive Summary |
| **Low Trust** | Cultural / Team Buy-in | Hero's Journey |
