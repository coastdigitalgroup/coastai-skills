# Example: Optimizing a B2B SaaS Interactive Tour

This case study shows how a CRM software company optimized their "Interactive
Product Tour" to increase trial signups.

## The Problem (Before)

The company had an interactive tour built with a 3rd party tool. While it saw
a lot of "starts," the conversion to free trials was low (~1.5%).

**Audit Findings:**
- **Length:** 18 steps.
- **Narrative:** The tour started with "Account Settings" and "User Management."
- **Copy:** "Click here to see the filter menu." (Tool-focused).
- **CTA:** A generic "Contact Us" at the end.
- **Drop-off:** 70% of users dropped off by Step 5.

## The Optimization (After)

Applying the **Interactive Demo Optimization** skill, the team refactored the
tour.

**Changes Made:**
- **Cut the Fluff:** Reduced the tour from 18 steps to 7 high-impact steps.
- **Outcome-First Start:** The tour now started on the "Automated Reports"
  dashboard (the core "Aha!" moment).
- **Benefit-Driven Copy:** Changed tooltips from "This is the filter" to "Find
  your highest-value leads in seconds."
- **Interactive Investment:** Replaced 3 "Next" clicks with a required "Type
  your goal" field to make the user feel more involved.
- **Alignment CTA:** Replaced "Contact Us" with a "Start My Free Trial" button
  that linked directly to the signup page with a "Demo Completed" flag.

## The Outcome

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Demo Completion Rate | 12% | 48% | +300% |
| Demo-to-Trial Conversion | 1.5% | 6.2% | +313% |
| Avg. Time in Demo | 45s | 120s | +166% |

**Key Insight:** By starting where the value is and cutting the "setup" steps,
the user's curiosity was maintained until the final conversion point.
