# Return Visitor Friction Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to identify where your website is losing momentum by
treating returning users like strangers.

## 1. Visual Continuity & Messaging
- [ ] **Adaptive Hero:** Does the homepage headline change for users who have
  visited 3+ times or viewed a specific high-intent page (e.g., Pricing)?
- [ ] **First-Timer Suppression:** Are "New Visitor" pop-ups, tutorials, or
  discounts hidden for users who have already seen them?
- [ ] **Interest Reflection:** Does the homepage or landing page surface
  content related to the user's previously browsed categories?

## 2. Transactional Persistence
- [ ] **Cart Longevity:** Does the guest cart persist for at least 7–14 days?
- [ ] **Recently Viewed:** Is there a "Recently Viewed" section on the home or
  product pages that works without logging in?
- [ ] **Last Search Recovery:** Does the search bar suggest the user's most
  recent queries when they tap/click it?

## 3. Progress & State Recovery
- [ ] **Form Persistence:** If a user partially fills a multi-step form and
  leaves, is their data still there when they return?
- [ ] **Video/Content Resume:** If the user was watching a demo video or
  reading a long-form guide, does the site remember their scroll/play position?
- [ ] **Tab/Selection Memory:** Does the site remember the user's last selected
  toggle (e.g., Monthly vs. Annual billing, or Metric vs. Imperial units)?

## 4. Psychological Triggers (The "Continuity" Hook)
- [ ] **The Zeigarnik Bar:** Is there a visible progress indicator for an
  unfinished high-value task (e.g., "Your quote is 80% complete")?
- [ ] **Recognition Widget:** Is there a "Pick Up Where You Left Off" link
  visible "above the fold" for returners?
- [ ] **Risk Reversal Persistence:** If the user viewed a "Guarantee" or
  "Safety" section previously, is that trust signal reinforced on their return?

## 5. Technical & Privacy Hygiene
- [ ] **Local Storage Usage:** Are you leveraging `localStorage` or `sessionStorage`
  for non-sensitive persistence to reduce server calls?
- [ ] **Reset Option:** Is there an easy way for the user to "Clear History" or
  "Reset View" to maintain a sense of control?
- [ ] **Compliance Check:** Does your persistence strategy align with your
  current Cookie Policy and GDPR/CCPA settings?
