# Customer Case Study Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a case study is optimized for conversion or merely acting as a "success story."

## 1. Title & Value Proposition
- [ ] Does the title lead with a measurable result (e.g., "Increased X by Y%")?
- [ ] Is the customer's name and industry clear from the headline?
- [ ] Does the title address a specific pain point of your target persona?

## 2. The Executive Summary (The "Skimmer" Test)
- [ ] Is there a summary block (sidebar or top section) that can be read in < 15 seconds?
- [ ] Does the summary include the Problem, Solution, and primary Result?
- [ ] Are the primary results/metrics visually distinct (e.g., large font, bold colors)?

## 3. Narrative Structure (The PSR Framework)
- [ ] **Problem:** Is the business pain clearly defined? Does it describe the "cost of inaction"?
- [ ] **Solution:** Does it explain *why* your solution was the right fit, rather than just listing features?
- [ ] **Result:** Are there specific numbers, percentages, or time-frames?
- [ ] **Quotability:** Is there at least one high-impact quote from a person with a relevant job title?

## 4. Visual Evidence & Trust
- [ ] Is there a high-quality logo of the customer?
- [ ] Is there a photo of the person being quoted?
- [ ] Are there screenshots, charts, or "before vs. after" visuals that validate the claims?
- [ ] Is the layout scannable with descriptive H2/H3 headers?

## 5. Path to Conversion
- [ ] Is there a clear, contextual Call to Action (CTA)?
- [ ] Does the CTA link to a relevant next step (Demo, Pricing, or related content)?
- [ ] Is the CTA placed at the end of the narrative *and* potentially in a sidebar/mid-page?
