# Customer Case Study Optimization

## Purpose

The Customer Case Study Optimization skill provides a systematic framework for
turning passive testimonials into persuasive conversion tools. Most case studies
fail because they are too long, too "company-centric," and lack clear,
measurable results. This skill uses the **Problem-Solution-Result (PSR)**
framework to establish empathy through business impact, demonstrate technical
capability, and provide quantifiable proof that drives the next step in the
buyer journey.

## Use Cases

- **B2B SaaS:** Demonstrating ROI for complex software solutions.
- **Service Agencies:** Showing the impact of strategic consulting or creative
  work.
- **High-Ticket Sales:** Reducing purchase anxiety for expensive, long-term
  commitments.
- **Sales Enablement:** Providing the sales team with "proof assets" that match
  specific prospect objections.

## When NOT to Use

- **Low-Complexity Consumer Goods:** For simple, low-friction purchases (e.g.,
  socks), standard reviews and social proof are more efficient.
- **Pre-Launch/Early Alpha:** If you don't have real customers with measurable
  outcomes yet, focus on `hero-section-optimization` or `value-calculator-optimization`.
- **Anonymized Success Stories:** Case studies without a named customer or
  identifiable industry are significantly less effective; focus on generic
  `social-proof-optimization` instead.

## Inputs

1. **Raw Customer Data:** Interviews, survey responses, or project completion
   reports.
2. **Measurable Metrics:** Specific KPIs that improved (e.g., "Saved 20 hours/week,"
   "Increased revenue by 15%").
3. **Target Persona:** Who are we trying to convince? What specific problems do
   they have?
4. **Primary Conversion Goal:** What is the specific action the user should take
   after reading? (e.g., "Book a Demo," "Start Free Trial").

## Outputs

1. **Optimized PSR Narrative:** A structured story focusing on the customer's
   transformation.
2. **Outcome Highlights:** High-visibility "stats bars" or pull-quotes that
   summarize the win.
3. **Strategic CTA Map:** Placement of contextual calls-to-action within the
   case study flow.
4. **Visual Information Architecture:** Guidance on using headers, bolding, and
   media to make the case study scannable.

## Workflow

### 1. The Narrative Audit (The PSR Filter)

Analyze the existing case study (or raw data) and filter it through the
Problem-Solution-Result framework:
- **Problem:** What was the specific business pain? (e.g., "Manual data entry
  took 40 hours a week").
- **Solution:** How was the product/service applied specifically to that pain?
- **Result:** What was the quantifiable outcome? (e.g., "90% reduction in entry
  time").

### 2. Radical Scannability

Users rarely read case studies word-for-word. Optimize for the "skimmer":
- **The "Outcome Header":** Replace generic titles like "Case Study: Acme Corp"
  with the result: "How Acme Corp Saved $50k in 3 Months."
- **The Stats Bar:** Create a 3-column summary at the very top showing the
  most impressive metrics.
- **Bold the "Wins":** Use bolding to highlight specific numbers and key
  benefits throughout the text.

### 3. Empathy-Driven Solutioning

Don't just list features. Explain the "How" and the "Why":
- **The Pivot Point:** Describe the moment the customer realized they needed a
  change.
- **Implementation Reality:** Be honest about the process to build trust.
- **Customer Voice:** Use direct quotes that sound like a real human, not a
  marketing script.

### 4. Conversion Pathing

A case study is a funnel, not just an article.
- **Contextual CTAs:** Instead of just one button at the bottom, place "micro-CTAs"
  after high-impact result sections (e.g., "See how it works for your team").
- **Related Cases:** Suggest 2 other case studies from the same industry or
  solving the same problem at the bottom.

### 5. Validation Setup

Define and implement the measurement plan to prove impact:
- **Baseline:** Record current case study conversion rates and scroll depth.
- **Event Tracking:** Ensure "Contextual CTA" clicks are tracked separately from
  the main footer CTA.
- **A/B Test (Optional):** If traffic allows, run the optimized PSR version
  against the original "company-centric" version.

### 6. Review Against Decision Rules

Ensure the narrative remains customer-focused and the results are prominent.

## Decision Rules

- **Customer as Hero:** The customer must be the protagonist; your product is
  simply the "mentor" or tool they used to win.
- **Numbers Over Adjectives:** Use "32% increase" instead of "significant
  improvement."
- **The "So What?" Test:** Every paragraph must either establish a problem,
  explain a solution, or prove a result. If it doesn't, cut it.
- **Proximity of Proof:** Place the most impressive testimonial quote directly
  next to the section describing the biggest result.

## Constraints

- **Verifiable Data:** All claims and metrics must be backed by actual customer data or verified project outcomes.
- **Customer Permission:** Final optimized content must be approved by the customer featured to ensure accuracy and maintain the relationship.
- **Brand Guidelines:** Narrative style and visual highlights must align with the existing brand voice and design system.

## Non-Goals

- Customer interview orchestration or raw data collection.
- Video production or high-end graphic design (though visual layout guidance is provided).
- Long-term customer relationship management.

## Common Failure Patterns

- **The "Wall of Text":** Long, unformatted paragraphs that no one reads.
- **The Feature Dump:** Listing every tool the customer used instead of focusing
  on the ones that solved the problem.
- **Vague Results:** Using corporate jargon like "optimized synergy" instead of
  real metrics.
- **Missing CTAs:** Letting the user reach the end of the story with no clear
  next step.
- **No Conflict:** Making it sound like everything was perfect from day one (no
  one believes this; it lacks the "drama" that builds trust).

## Validation methods

- [ ] **Case Study Conversion Rate:** The percentage of readers who click the
  primary CTA within the page.
- [ ] **Scroll Depth:** Measure how far down the page users go. If they drop off
  before the "Result," the "Problem" section is too long.
- [ ] **Time on Page:** An increase in time on page for case studies often
  correlates with higher-quality leads in B2B.
- [ ] **Lead Quality:** Track if users who view a case study convert at a higher
  rate at the final checkout/demo stage than those who don't.
