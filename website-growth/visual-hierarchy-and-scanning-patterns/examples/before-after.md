# Example: Optimizing a SaaS Landing Page Hierarchy

This example shows how a SaaS company optimized their landing page to move from
a "Wall of Text" to a "Layer-Cake" scanning pattern, resulting in higher
engagement and conversion.

## Scenario: The "Boring" Landing Page

**Company:** CloudSync (B2B File Management)

**The Problem:** The landing page had high traffic from ads, but 65% of users
bounced within 10 seconds. Heatmaps showed users scrolled past the "Features"
section without stopping or clicking any links.

### Before: The "Information Dump"
- **Hero Section:** A small headline "CloudSync is the best for your business"
  followed by 4 lines of small body text. The CTA button was light grey.
- **Features Section:** A single block of 500 words explaining the technology.
  No subheadings, no icons, and no bolding.
- **Social Proof:** Logos were buried at the very bottom of the page.
- **Scanning Pattern:** Users tried to use the **F-Pattern** but became
  exhausted by the lack of "hooks" and left (the "Wall of Text" failure).

### After: The Optimized "Layer-Cake" Flow
- **Hero Section:**
  - **Hierarchy:** H1 was tripled in size ("Sync 1TB in 10 Seconds").
  - **Contrast:** The CTA was changed to a high-contrast Brand Orange.
  - **Directional Cue:** A screenshot of the app was placed on the right, with
    the interface pointing toward the "Start Free Trial" button.
- **Features Section:**
  - **Layer-Cake Pattern:** The long block of text was broken into 3 distinct
    sections with large H2 subheadings (e.g., "Enterprise-Grade Security,"
    "Instant Team Sync").
  - **Spotted Pattern:** Key technical specs (e.g., "AES-256") were **bolded**
    to catch the eye of technical buyers.
- **Social Proof:** A logo bar of "Trusted by Fortune 500" was moved
  immediately below the Hero section to establish trust early in the scan.

## Measurable Outcome

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bounce Rate** | 65% | 42% | -35% |
| **Avg. Scroll Depth** | 22% | 58% | +163% |
| **CTA Click-Through Rate** | 1.8% | 4.2% | +133% |
| **Demo Requests** | 12 / week | 28 / week | +133% |

## Key Lesson
By breaking the "Wall of Text" and implementing a **Layer-Cake** scanning
pattern, the company allowed users to "snack" on the benefits that mattered to
them. The increased **visual contrast** on the CTA ensured that once the user
was convinced, the "Next Step" was impossible to miss.
