# Video Conversion Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to identify friction and conversion gaps in your website's
video assets.

## 1. Packaging (The "Play" Decision)
- [ ] **Thumbnail Contrast:** Does the thumbnail stand out against the page
      background?
- [ ] **Human Element:** If appropriate, does the thumbnail feature a person's
      face (improves trust and CTR)?
- [ ] **Value Copy:** Does the thumbnail include 3-5 words of "Hook" text that
      promises a benefit?
- [ ] **Play Button Visibility:** Is there a clear, high-contrast "Play" icon
      centered on the thumbnail?
- [ ] **Title Alignment:** Does the video title mirror the primary keyword or
      pain point of the page?

## 2. The Hook (The First 10 Seconds)
- [ ] **Instant Value:** Does the content start immediately (no logo animations
      or "Hi, I'm...")?
- [ ] **Problem Validation:** Does the video state the user's problem within the
      first 5 seconds?
- [ ] **Sound-Off Clarity:** Are there captions or large text overlays
      explaining the core message without audio?
- [ ] **Visual Velocity:** Is there a visual change (cut, movement, text) at
      least every 3-5 seconds in the intro?

## 3. Persuasive Content
- [ ] **Benefit Over Feature:** Does the video show the *result* of using the
      product, not just the interface?
- [ ] **Social Proof:** Are there snippets of testimonials, logos, or "Used by
      X people" included?
- [ ] **Objection Handling:** Does the video address at least one major "Why
      NOT to buy" concern?
- [ ] **Logical Length:** Is the video as short as possible while still
      delivering the necessary proof?

## 4. The Conversion Path
- [ ] **One-Goal CTA:** Is there a single, clear instruction at the end of the
      video?
- [ ] **On-Page Proximity:** Is there a conversion button within 100px of the
      video player?
- [ ] **Mid-Roll/End-Screen Overlays:** Are there interactive elements to
      capture the user's interest before they close the player?
- [ ] **Contextual Relevance:** Does the "Ask" in the video match the user's
      current stage (e.g., don't ask for a purchase in a "Top of Funnel" video)?

## 5. Technical Performance
- [ ] **LCP Impact:** Is the video (or its poster image) optimized so it doesn't
      tank the Largest Contentful Paint score?
- [ ] **Mobile Touch:** Are the player controls large enough for thumb
      interaction?
- [ ] **Autoplay Ethics:** If the video autoplays, is it muted by default and
      does it respect `prefers-reduced-motion`?
