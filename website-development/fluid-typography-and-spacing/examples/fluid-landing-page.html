Fluid vs. Steppy

Resize your browser to see the difference in how the typography and spacing behave.

Traditional Media Queries

Responsive Growth

This side uses standard media queries. Watch the heading closely as you resize. It "jumps" abruptly at 768px and 1200px. Between these points, the typography remains static, which often creates "too small" or "too large" awkward phases on specific device sizes (like large tablets).

Fluid clamp() Implementation

Responsive Growth

This side uses clamp(). The heading and the gap above this paragraph scale linearly with every single pixel of viewport width. There are no jumps. The design maintains its proportional harmony at 450px, 800px, and 1100px widths automatically.

The "Why"

In the Traditional approach, we had to write 6 lines of CSS and 2 media queries just for the heading.

In the Fluid approach, we defined a single variable and applied it once. This leads to cleaner code and a more polished "app-like" feel to websites.
