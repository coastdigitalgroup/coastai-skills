# Example: B2B SaaS Homepage Optimization

## The Scenario
A B2B SaaS company, "BuildTrack," provides project management software for
commercial construction. Their primary goal is to get users to book a 30-minute
live sales demo.

### The Problem
- **Primary CTA:** "Book a Demo"
- **Primary Conversion Rate:** 0.8%
- **Bounce Rate:** 72%
- **User Insight:** Post-exit surveys indicated that many visitors liked the
  value proposition but felt that a 30-minute call was too much of a commitment
  when they were just starting their research. They wanted to "see the tool"
  without the pressure of a sales conversation.

## The Optimization: Adding a Secondary CTA

The team implemented a Secondary CTA (SCTA) to provide a lower-friction path to
value.

### Before
- **Hero Section:** One primary "Book a Demo" button (Solid Blue).
- **Secondary Action:** None. Users who weren't ready for a call simply left.

### After
- **Hero Section:** Primary "Book a Demo" button (Solid Blue) remains.
- **Added SCTA:** A "Watch 2-Min Product Tour" button (Ghost/Outline style) was
  placed immediately next to the primary button.
- **Strategy:** The 2-minute video provides immediate visual proof of the
  product's value without requiring any user data or time commitment beyond the
  video duration.

## The Results (Measured over 30 days)

| Metric | Before SCTA | After SCTA | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary CVR (Demo Booked)** | 0.8% | 0.78% | -2.5% (Negligible) |
| **Secondary CVR (Video View)** | 0% | 4.2% | +4.2% |
| **Total Lead Capture** | 0.8% | 4.98% | **+522%** |
| **Bounce Rate** | 72% | 61% | -15% |
| **Assisted Conversions** | N/A | 12% | 12% of demo bookings now come from users who first watched the video. |

## Why it Worked
1. **Reduced Anxiety:** Users who were "Demo-Averse" found a way to satisfy
   their curiosity without "talking to a human."
2. **Visual Subordination:** By using a ghost button for the "Watch Tour," the
   "Book a Demo" button still looked like the "correct" next step for
   high-intent users, preventing significant cannibalization.
3. **Intent Match:** The "Product Tour" was a perfect stepping stone. Once a
   user saw the video, they were *more* likely to feel that a 30-minute deep
   dive was worth their time.
