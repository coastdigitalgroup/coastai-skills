# Example: Optimizing the Cart Experience for a Multi-Item E-commerce Store

This example shows how applying the Cart Experience Optimization skill transformed
a high-abandonment cart into a high-conversion gateway for an outdoor gear
retailer.

## Before: The "High-Anxiety" Cart

The retailer noticed that while many users added items to their cart, only 35%
began the checkout process.

### The Situation
- **Cart Type:** Full-page cart that pulled users away from their shopping flow.
- **Cost Transparency:** Only the subtotal was shown. Shipping and taxes were
  hidden until the final checkout step.
- **CTA:** A small, grey "Proceed to Checkout" button at the bottom of a long list
  of products.
- **Mobile Experience:** Users had to scroll past a long list of individual
  items and upsells to find the checkout button.
- **Distractions:** A prominent "Enter Coupon Code" box led users to leave the
  site to search for discounts.

### Measurable Outcome
- **Cart-to-Checkout Rate:** 35%
- **Average Time in Cart:** 2m 15s (indicating confusion/comparison)
- **Support Volume:** High number of "How much is shipping?" queries.

---

## After: The "Frictionless" Gateway

Following the Cart Experience Optimization protocol, the site implemented
the following changes.

### The Fix
1. **Slide-Out Cart:** Replaced the full-page cart with a persistent slide-out
   drawer, allowing users to see their progress without losing their place on
   the product page.
2. **Shipping Threshold Tracker:** Added a visual progress bar: "You're only $15
   away from Free Shipping!"
3. **Zip Code Estimator:** Included a small input for the zip code to provide
   real-time shipping and tax estimates directly in the cart.
4. **Primary CTA Redesign:** Used a high-contrast bright orange button for
   "Checkout Now," and made it sticky at the bottom of the mobile drawer.
5. **Express Pay Integration:** Added one-click Apple Pay and Shop Pay buttons
   above the manual checkout button.
6. **Coupon De-emphasis:** Moved the promo code field behind a "Have a discount?"
   link to reduce exit-intent.

### Measurable Outcome
- **Cart-to-Checkout Rate:** 52% (a 48% relative increase)
- **Average Order Value (AOV):** Increased by 12% due to the shipping threshold
  tracker.
- **Mobile Conversion Rate:** 18% lift in mobile checkout starts.
- **Support Volume:** 40% decrease in shipping-related pre-sale questions.

## Summary of Impact
By prioritizing cost transparency and reducing interaction cost, the retailer
turned the cart from a point of hesitation into a high-momentum bridge to the
sale.
