# Cart Design Heuristics & Principles

Optimizing the cart experience requires balancing business goals (like AOV)
with user psychological needs (like certainty and control).

## 1. The Rule of Transparency
Users are most likely to abandon a purchase when they encounter "new"
information late in the funnel. The cart should be the "Truth Layer" where
all costs—product, shipping, and estimated tax—are revealed.
*   **Action:** Provide zip-code based estimators to pull "Checkout" information
    forward into the "Cart" phase.

## 2. The Frictionless Path
The transition from "Interested" to "Buying" is a high-anxiety moment. Any
technical or cognitive hurdle can trigger a "maybe later" response.
*   **Action:** Prioritize "Express" payment methods (Apple Pay, Google Pay) to
    reduce the number of fields a user must interact with by up to 90%.

## 3. The Endowment Effect (Managing Removal)
When a user adds an item to their cart, they begin to feel a sense of ownership.
Removing an item is psychologically "painful."
*   **Action:** Instead of just a "Delete" button, offer a "Save for Later"
    or "Move to Wishlist" option. This keeps the item in the user's peripheral
    vision without cluttering the current purchase.

## 4. Choice Architecture in Upsells
While the cart is a great place to increase AOV, too many choices can lead to
"Decision Fatigue," causing the user to abandon the entire cart.
*   **Action:** Limit "Frequently Bought Together" items to no more than 3
    highly relevant accessories. Never suggest unrelated products.

## 5. The Progress Principle
Users are more likely to complete a task if they feel they have already made
significant progress.
*   **Action:** Use progress bars for shipping thresholds or "Order Complete"
    steps. Seeing that they are "80% of the way to free shipping" motivates
    further action.

## 6. Mobile Ergonomics (The Thumb Zone)
Mobile users interact with the cart while on-the-go or with one hand. The
most important buttons must be within easy reach.
*   **Action:** Use sticky footers for the "Checkout" button on mobile. Ensure
    all "Remove" or "Update" links are large enough to avoid mis-taps.
