# Growth Heuristics for 404 Recovery

Optimizing a 404 page is about managing disappointment and redirecting energy. Follow these psychological and UX principles to maximize recovery.

## 1. The Principle of Least Effort
When a user hits a 404, they have just experienced a "failure" of the interface. Their patience is at its lowest.
- **Heuristic:** Don't make them think. Provide the most likely solution (Search or Home) as the path of least resistance.

## 2. Information Scent Persistence
Users were looking for something specific when they clicked. Even if the page is gone, the "scent" of their intent remains.
- **Heuristic:** If the URL contains keywords (e.g., "shoes"), use those keywords to populate the recovery recommendations. This maintains the "Scent of Information."

## 3. The Zeigarnik Effect (Closure)
Users have an interrupted task. They wanted to see a product or read a post.
- **Heuristic:** Use language that promises closure. "We couldn't find that exact article, but here is the updated 2024 version."

## 4. Brand Empathy and "Pratfall Effect"
Admitting a mistake can actually make a brand more likable (the Pratfall Effect), provided the brand is generally perceived as competent.
- **Heuristic:** Use a tone that is human and helpful. A small joke or a humble apology reduces the user's frustration and builds "Moral Capital."

## 5. Decision Paralysis (Hick's Law)
While you want to be helpful, providing 50 links to every page on your site will overwhelm the user.
- **Heuristic:** Limit recovery options to the "Vital Few." One search bar and 3-5 high-value category links is more effective than a full sitemap.

## 6. The "Risk Reversal" of an Error
A 404 is a moment of high friction.
- **Heuristic:** Counteract the friction with an unexpected "Gift." A small discount or a high-value freebie transforms a negative experience into a positive "Surprise and Delight" moment.
