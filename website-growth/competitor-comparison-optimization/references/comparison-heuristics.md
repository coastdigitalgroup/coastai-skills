# Competitor Comparison Heuristics

These heuristics are grounded in cognitive psychology and conversion rate
optimization (CRO) principles. Use them to guide the design and copy of
comparison pages.

## 1. The Framing Effect
The way information is presented ("framed") significantly influences decisions.
- **Heuristic:** Don't just list what you have; frame the competitor's
  strengths as weaknesses for your specific target audience.
- _Example:_ If a competitor has "1,000+ Features," frame it as "Feature Bloat
  that slows down your team."

## 2. Anchoring and Adjustment
Users rely heavily on the first piece of information offered (the "anchor").
- **Heuristic:** If you are cheaper, anchor on the competitor's high price
  first. If you are more expensive, anchor on the "Cost of Failure" or the
  "ROI of Quality" before showing the price.

## 3. The "Honest Advantage" Principle
Absolute perfection is suspicious. Admitting minor weaknesses increases the
credibility of your major strengths.
- **Heuristic:** Explicitly state who should NOT use your product.
- _Example:_ "If you are a 5,000+ person enterprise needing legacy COBOL
  support, [Competitor] is the better choice. If you are a modern team of 50
  needing speed, choose us."

## 4. Feature-Benefit Translation
Users don't buy features; they buy a "Better Version of Themselves."
- **Heuristic:** Every row in a comparison table should answer the user's
  internal question: "What does this actually do for my day-to-day?"
- _Technical:_ "SOC2 Type II Certified"
- _Benefit:_ "Sleep better knowing your customer data is enterprise-secure."

## 5. The Zeigarnik Effect (The "Unfinished Business")
The psychological tendency to remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better
than completed ones.
- **Heuristic:** Highlight the "Work" the competitor forces on the user. Show
  how your product "Closes the Loop" faster.
- _Example:_ "Stop spending 4 hours a week on manual data entry in
  [Competitor]. FlowTask automates it in 10 seconds."

## 6. Sunk Cost Fallacy Neutralization
Users are afraid to switch because of the time/money already invested in the
competitor.
- **Heuristic:** Proactively address the "Pain of Switching." Use visual
  evidence (videos/GIFs) of the migration process to show it is a "Low-Calorie"
  task.

## 7. Social Default (Bandwagon Effect)
People tend to follow the lead of others, especially "people like them."
- **Heuristic:** Use "Switcher Personas." Group testimonials by the tool they
  left (e.g., "Why 500+ companies left [Competitor] for us this year").
