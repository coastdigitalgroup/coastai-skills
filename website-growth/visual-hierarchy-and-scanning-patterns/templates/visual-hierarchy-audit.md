# Visual Hierarchy & Scanning Audit Template

Use this checklist to evaluate the attention-management of any landing page,
blog post, or product page.

## Phase 1: The "Gut Check" (Immediate Perception)

- [ ] **The Squint Test:** Squint at the page. Is the most important element
  (the CTA) still one of the top 3 visible items?
- [ ] **The 5-Second Test:** Show the page to someone for 5 seconds. Can they
  tell you exactly what the product is and what they should do next?
- [ ] **First Focal Point:** Is the first thing a user sees the Value
  Proposition (Top-Left or Center)?

## Phase 2: Scanning Pattern Alignment

- [ ] **Scanning Pattern Choice:** Is the layout intentionally designed for
  one of the following? (Select one)
  - [ ] **Z-Pattern** (Simple Landing Page)
  - [ ] **F-Pattern** (Informational/Article)
  - [ ] **Layer-Cake** (Feature List/Service Page)
  - [ ] **Spotted** (Data/Spec Sheet)
- [ ] **Horizontal Hooks:** If using F or Layer-cake, do the headlines (H2/H3)
  start with benefit-driven words rather than generic filler?
- [ ] **Visual Breaks:** Are there visual shifts (background color, images,
  quotes) at least every 2-3 screen heights to prevent "Scroll Fatigue"?

## Phase 3: The Mechanics of Hierarchy

### Typography & Scale
- [ ] **Clear Ratios:** Is there a visible size difference between H1, H2,
  and Body text? (H1 should be at least 2x body size).
- [ ] **Font Weight:** Are H2s bold or heavier than the body text to act as
  anchor points for the eye?

### Contrast & Color
- [ ] **The "Pop" Element:** Does the primary CTA have a unique color not
  used elsewhere for non-interactive elements?
- [ ] **Negative Space:** Is there at least 40-80px of whitespace around the
  primary Value Proposition and CTA?

### Directional Cues
- [ ] **Eye Paths:** If there are photos of people, are they looking toward
  the CTA or the headline?
- [ ] **Visual Anchors:** Are icons or bullet points used to "pull" the eye
  through a list of benefits?

## Phase 4: Mobile Prioritization

- [ ] **Vertical Stack:** Does the hierarchy hold up when elements are
  stacked vertically?
- [ ] **Priority Re-order:** Is the most important information moved to the
  top of the stack on mobile (even if it was on the right on desktop)?
- [ ] **Touch Targets:** Are all "points of attention" also easy to tap
  (min 44x44px)?
