# Example: Exit-Intent Recovery Optimization

This example demonstrates how a B2B SaaS company selling project management
software used exit-intent recovery to turn "bouncing" traffic into qualified
leads.

## Scenario: The "Feature-Aware" Abandoner

A SaaS company noticed that 85% of visitors to their "Features" page left
without clicking "Start Free Trial." These visitors were "Solution Aware" (they
knew what project management tools were) but weren't ready to commit to a trial
immediately.

### The "Before" State

- **Exit Path:** Users browsed features, reached the bottom of the page, and
  simply closed the tab or navigated back to search results.
- **Conversion Rate:** 2.1% (Direct to Free Trial).
- **Missed Opportunity:** Thousands of potential customers were leaving without
  the company having any way to follow up or provide further value.

### The Optimization (The "After")

The company implemented a context-aware Exit-Intent strategy:

1. **The Trigger:**
   - Detection of mouse movement toward the browser's "Close" button or "Back"
     button on desktop.
   - Detection of a rapid "Scroll Up" on mobile after the user had read at least
     50% of the page.

2. **The Offer (The "Content Upgrade"):**
   - Instead of pushing the "Free Trial" again, the company offered a high-value
     lead magnet: _"The Remote Team Productivity Checklist: 15 Habits of
     High-Performing Teams."_

3. **The Design:**
   - A clean, centered modal that appeared only when the exit signal was
     detected.
   - Headline: "Wait! Before you go, grab our Remote Team Checklist."
   - Visual: A small preview image of the PDF checklist.
   - CTA: "Send Me the Checklist" (Only required an Email address).

### Measurable Outcomes

| Metric                       | Before | After  | Change             |
| :--------------------------- | :----- | :----- | :----------------- |
| **Direct Trial Conversion**  | 2.1%   | 2.0%   | -0.1% (Negligible) |
| **Exit-Intent Lead Capture** | 0%     | 6.5%   | **+6.5%**          |
| **Total Lead Volume**        | 210/mo | 860/mo | **+309.5%**        |
| **Nurture-to-Trial Rate**    | N/A    | 12%    | **+12%**           |

## Key Takeaways

- **Lower Friction:** The "Checklist" required much less commitment than a "Free
  Trial" with account setup.
- **Micro-Conversion:** By capturing the email address at the point of exit, the
  company could "nurture" the user via email, eventually leading to more trials
  over time.
- **Contextual Alignment:** The checklist was directly related to
  "Productivity," which is why the user was on the "Features" page in the first
  place.
