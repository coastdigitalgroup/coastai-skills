# Example: Upsell & Cross-sell Optimization

This example demonstrates how an e-commerce brand selling high-end coffee
equipment optimized their funnel to increase Average Order Value (AOV).

## Scenario: Premium Coffee Roaster

A specialty coffee brand sells a popular "Barista Home Kit" for $250. While
sales are steady, the AOV is stagnant because most customers only buy the kit
and nothing else.

### The "Before" State

- **Current AOV:** $250.00
- **Cross-sell Strategy:** None (Users go straight from PDP to Cart to Checkout).
- **Missed Opportunity:** Customers often realize later they need filters, a
  scale, or fresh beans, but they don't buy them during the initial transaction.

### The Optimization (The "After")

The brand implemented three strategic "Value Triggers":

1.  **In-Cart Cross-sell (The "Essential Utility"):**
    - **Trigger:** When the Barista Kit is added to the cart.
    - **Offer:** A pack of 100 compostable filters for $15 (normally $20).
    - **Logic:** "Don't forget filters! Add to cart for 25% off."
2.  **PDP Upsell (The "Better/Best" Anchor):**
    - **Trigger:** On the Barista Kit product page.
    - **Offer:** A comparison table showing the "Pro Kit" ($350) which includes
      a built-in grinder.
    - **Logic:** "Upgrade to the Pro Kit and save $50 compared to buying the
      grinder separately."
3.  **Post-Purchase One-Click Upsell (The "Enhanced Experience"):**
    - **Trigger:** Immediately after the "Order Complete" button is clicked.
    - **Offer:** A 3-month coffee bean subscription for $45 (30% off the first
      month).
    - **Logic:** "Your gear is on the way! Want the world's best beans to go
      with it? Add to your order with one click."

### Measurable Outcomes

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | $250.00 | $295.50 | **+18.2%** |
| **Conversion Rate (Primary)** | 3.2% | 3.1% | -0.1% (Negligible) |
| **Cross-sell Take Rate** | 0% | 14% | **+14%** |
| **Revenue Per Visitor (RPV)** | $8.00 | $9.16 | **+14.5%** |

## Key Takeaways

- **Low Friction:** The filter cross-sell was a "no-brainer" because it cost
  less than 10% of the primary item.
- **Timing:** The bean subscription was offered *after* the primary sale was
  secured, ensuring it didn't distract the user during the checkout process.
- **One-Click:** By using one-click technology for the post-purchase offer, the
  brand eliminated the friction of re-entering payment details, leading to a
  high subscription take-rate.
