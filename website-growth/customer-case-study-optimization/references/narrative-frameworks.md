# Narrative Frameworks for Conversion

To optimize a case study, you must understand the psychological principles that
make a story persuasive. These frameworks go beyond "marketing copy" and tap
into how humans process information and build trust.

## 1. The PSR Framework (Problem-Solution-Result)

This is the foundational logic of high-conversion case studies.
- **Problem (The Pain):** Establish empathy. If the reader doesn't feel the
  pain, the solution has no value.
- **Solution (The Bridge):** Demonstrate technical competency. Show *how* the
  work was done.
- **Result (The Proof):** Provide the dopamine hit of success. Use hard numbers
  to make the success "real."

## 2. The Hero’s Journey (B2B Adaptation)

In a successful case study, the **Customer is the Hero**, and your brand is the
**Mentor (The Guide)**.
1. **The Call to Adventure:** The customer realizes their current process (Excel,
   manual labor, etc.) is failing.
2. **Meeting the Mentor:** The customer discovers your product/service.
3. **Crossing the Threshold:** The implementation/onboarding phase.
4. **The Ordeal:** Addressing the specific challenges (e.g., data migration,
   team pushback).
5. **The Reward:** Achieving the measurable results (the "Win").

## 3. Social Proof & Authority Bias

Humans are biologically wired to follow the "wisdom of the crowd," especially
when the crowd looks like them.
- **Relatability:** Prospects are most convinced by customers who share their
  industry, company size, and job title.
- **Outcome Bias:** We tend to judge a decision based on its eventual outcome.
  A detailed "Result" section justifies the prospect’s desire to purchase.

## 4. The Peak-End Rule

Psychological research shows that people judge an experience largely based on
how they felt at its **peak** (the biggest result) and at its **end** (the CTA).
- **The Peak:** Ensure the most impressive metric or quote is visually dominant.
- **The End:** Ensure the final call-to-action is clear, encouraging, and
  logical.

## 5. SCQA (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer)

A framework for technical and executive storytelling:
- **Situation:** "We were a $10M manufacturer using spreadsheets."
- **Complication:** "As we grew, we began losing 10% of our lead data."
- **Question:** "How could we scale without hiring 3 more admin staff?"
- **Answer:** "PipelinePro’s automation allowed us to grow 2x with the same
  team."
