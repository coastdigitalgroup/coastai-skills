# Narrative Frameworks for Case Studies

To maximize the conversion impact of a case study, use one of these proven narrative frameworks to structure your content.

## 1. The PSR (Problem-Solution-Result) Framework
The gold standard for B2B and SaaS case studies. It follows the natural logic of a business decision.

*   **Problem:** Start with the "villain" — the inefficiency, the lost revenue, or the manual hurdle. Quantify the pain.
*   **Solution:** Introduce the "weapon" — your product or service. Explain the *logic* of why it worked for this specific problem.
*   **Result:** Celebrate the "victory" — the measurable outcome. Use the "Proof, Not Promises" approach.

## 2. The "Before-During-After" Framework
Ideal for services or complex implementations where the *transition* is the most important part.

*   **Before:** What did the status quo look like? (The "Messy Middle").
*   **During:** What was the implementation process like? (Addressing the "Will this be hard to set up?" objection).
*   **After:** What is the new "business as usual"? What is the long-term ROI?

## 3. The "Industry Spotlight" Framework
Best for establishing authority in a specific vertical (e.g., Healthcare, FinTech).

*   **The Context:** What are the unique challenges of this industry?
*   **The Compliance/Regulatory hurdle:** How did the solution handle the specific constraints of the vertical?
*   **The Outcome:** How did this customer outperform their industry peers?

## 4. The "Feature Deep-Dive" Framework
Use this when you need to prove the value of a specific, high-intent feature.

*   **The Gap:** A specific task that was impossible or difficult before.
*   **The Feature in Action:** Screenshots and step-by-step logic of how the feature solved the task.
*   **The Outcome:** The specific productivity or accuracy lift from that one feature.
